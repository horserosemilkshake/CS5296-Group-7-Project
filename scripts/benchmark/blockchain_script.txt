Blockchain is a revolutionary distributed ledger technology (DLT) that provides a secure, transparent, and decentralized way to record transactions without relying on a central authority. Originally introduced in 2008 as the underlying foundation for Bitcoin, blockchain has since evolved into a versatile system for managing digital data, ownership, and smart contracts. At its core, data is stored in "blocks" linked together in chronological order, forming a continuous chain. These blocks contain batches of transactions validated by multiple network participants, or "nodes," using a consensus mechanism.

Once data is recorded on a blockchain, it becomes nearly impossible to alter or delete, offering a high degree of immutability and security against tampering. Unlike traditional centralized databases that act as a single point of failure, blockchain distributes the data across a network, making it highly resilient. While cryptocurrency is the most famous use case, blockchain technology is now transforming various industries, including supply chain management, healthcare, and finance, by enhancing trust, reducing fraud, and removing intermediaries. By utilizing public key cryptography, blockchain enables direct, secure digital asset exchanges.
